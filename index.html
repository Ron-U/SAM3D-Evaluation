A Comprehensive Evaluation of SAM-3D

First Author*, Second Author*, Third Author
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

With the rapid advancement of generative AI, single-image 3D reconstruction has transitioned from isolated object recovery to the holistic reconstruction of complex scenes. As a novel generative foundation model, SAM-3D enables the simultaneous inference of object geometry, high-fidelity texture, and spatial layout from a single natural image, demonstrating exceptional robustness in handling complex backgrounds and severe occlusions. Unlike traditional scene-level models constrained by specific training distributions, SAM-3D leverages large-scale high-quality data and a multi-stage training strategy to exhibit superior zero-shot generalization potential. In this work, we conduct a systematic empirical analysis of SAM-3D covering four core dimensions: geometric quality, texture fidelity, layout precision, and input diversity. Through extensive qualitative analysis and visual demonstrations, we highlight the model's significant advantages in cross-category generalization while delineating its limitations in fine-grained geometric detailing and spatial consistency, providing empirical references and directional guidance for the future optimization of SAM-3D.
